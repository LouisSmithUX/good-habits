Why use an online habit tracker?

You may have tried in the past to track your habits. You made a list of the best habits to track, found a printout calendar and hung it up on your fridge. If you were like me, you did it for a couple weeks and then the hassle of looking for a pen each morning was too much. One day you missed filling the calendar. Then you missed a couple of days, and before you knew it you weren’t tracking it at all anymore. And then you lost the habit.

You can break down a habit into a feedback loop with four steps: cue, craving, response & reward. The trick to stay commited is to have the cue not come from you but from an external source. If the cue doesn’t come from you, you don’t have to spend the extra cognitive energy reminding yourself to check your list of habits - it reminds you of what is left to do.

Remember habit tracking is not the same as goal setting. Continuous habits over years act like compound interest on your skills and abilities, much more than some ambitious but sparse objectives.
